Health & Safety Code: Controlled Substances

A Guide to Drug Laws, Mental Health, and Crisis Intervention

I. Mental Health Awareness for Officers

Defining Mental Illness & Insanity

Mental Illness: A diagnosable condition that impairs thought, behavior, or perception of reality. It's diagnosed by professionals using the DSM-5. Main categories include personality disorders, mood disorders, anxiety disorders, psychosis, and developmental disorders.

Insanity: A legal term, not a psychological one. It refers to a decreased ability to distinguish right from wrong at the time of a criminal act.

Common Disorders Encountered

  • Personality Disorders: Includes Paranoid (irrational mistrust), Antisocial (destructive patterns, often in males), and Borderline (unstable relationships, often in females).
  • Mood Disorders: Includes Depression (low energy, lack of interest, suicide risk) and Bipolar Disorder (cycles of manic highs and depressive lows).
  • Psychosis: Characterized by delusions (false beliefs) and hallucinations (faulty perceptions). Schizophrenia is a common psychotic disorder.
  • Developmental Disabilities: Lifelong impairments like Autism or Intellectual Disability. Individuals may be highly sensitive to touch, prone to false confessions, and require simple, slow communication.

Crisis Intervention (CIT) Principles

CIT was developed to help officers de-escalate situations with mentally ill individuals. Key principles include:

  • LEAPS Communication Model: Listen, Empathize, Ask, Paraphrase, Summarize.
  • Modeling: The officer embodies calm, rational behavior to influence the person in crisis.
  • Emergency Detention: Officers may detain a person without a warrant if they are mentally ill and pose a risk of harm to self or others. This person should be taken to a medical facility, not jail, except in emergencies.
  • Jail Diversion: A Texas initiative to separate the mentally ill from the criminal justice system, reducing costs, stigma, and violence.

II. The Texas Controlled Substances Act

Penalty Groups 1 & 1-A

These groups include the most dangerous and highly addictive substances, such as heroin, cocaine, and methamphetamine. LSD is in Group 1-A.

  • Manufacture/Delivery (Sec 481.112, 481.1121): Ranges from a state jail felony for small amounts to a first-degree felony or higher for large amounts, with potential life sentences and massive fines.
  • Possession (Sec 481.115, 481.1151): Ranges from a state jail felony for less than 1 gram (or <20 abuse units) to a first-degree felony or higher for large amounts.

Penalty Groups 2, 3, & 4

These groups include other controlled substances with varying potential for abuse, such as hallucinogens (Group 2), stimulants/depressants (Group 3), and narcotics with medical uses (Group 4).

  • Manufacture/Delivery (Sec 481.113, 481.114): Penalties scale with weight, starting at a state jail felony and increasing to first-degree or higher for large quantities.
  • Possession (Sec 481.116, 481.117, 481.118): Penalties vary significantly by group. Possession of a small amount of a Group 4 substance is a Class B misdemeanor, while a small amount of a Group 2 substance is a state jail felony.

Marijuana Offenses

Marijuana has its own penalty structure separate from the main penalty groups.

  • Possession (Sec 481.121): 2 ounces or less is a Class B misdemeanor. 2-4 ounces is a Class A misdemeanor. 4 ounces to 5 pounds is a state jail felony. Penalties increase significantly with weight.
  • Delivery (Sec 481.120): Delivering less than a quarter-ounce for no pay is a Class B misdemeanor. If for pay, it's a Class A misdemeanor. Penalties scale up to a first-degree felony or higher for large amounts.

Other Drug-Related Offenses

  • Dangerous Drugs (Ch. 483): Possession is a Class A misdemeanor. Delivery or manufacture is a state jail felony. Forging a prescription is a Class B misdemeanor.
  • Abusable Volatile Chemicals (Ch. 485): Possessing or using chemicals like aerosol paint for intoxication is a Class B misdemeanor. Selling them without a permit is also a Class B misdemeanor.
  • Drug Paraphernalia (Sec 481.125): Possession is a Class C misdemeanor. Delivery or manufacture is a Class A misdemeanor.
