Emergency Medical Assistance

First Responder Protocols and Procedures

I. Initial Assessment & Trauma Care

Patient Assessment & Bleeding Control

The first moments of a medical emergency are critical. Follow these steps in order:

  1. Ensure Scene Safety: Before approaching, confirm the location is safe for you and the victim. Relocate if necessary.
  2. Primary Survey (ABC):
    • Airway: Ascertain the airway is open and clear.
    • Breathing: Check for breathing.
    • Circulation: Check for a pulse and major bleeding.
  3. Bleeding Control:
    • Apply direct pressure with a clean dressing.
    • Elevate the wound above the heart if possible.
    • If direct pressure fails, apply pressure to the relevant artery (pressure point).
    • Obtain professional medical care as soon as possible.

Treating Common Injuries

Burns:

Remove the source of the burn, cool the area with water, and cover with a dry, clean bandage.

Shock:

Have the person lie down and elevate their legs (unless head/neck/spine injury is suspected). Provide comfort and DO NOT give food or drink.

Broken Bones/Sprains:

Immobilize the affected area immediately, applying a splint if appropriate. Look for numbness, swelling, bruising, and restricted mobility.

Poisoning and Environmental Exposure

  • Chemical Poisoning (skin): Immediately flush the affected area with water.
  • Ingested Poison: Check vital signs and seek immediate medical help. Do not give food or drink.
  • Snake/Spider Bites: Clean the wound and immobilize it below the level of the heart.
  • Cold Exposure: Move to a warm location, remove wet clothing, and apply dry blankets.
  • Heat Exposure: Move to a cool location, give water, loosen clothing, and apply cool, damp cloths to the skin.

II. Life-Saving Interventions & Special Cases

Choking, CPR, and Childbirth

Choking: Perform the Heimlich maneuver by delivering quick, upward thrusts with a fist just above the navel.

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation): After ensuring a clear airway, deliver breaths and chest compressions to circulate blood. For adults, deliver compressions to the center of the rib cage.

Emergency Childbirth: Support the baby's head as it emerges, then the shoulders and body. Immediately clear the baby's airway by swiping a finger across the back of the throat. Perform gentle CPR if the baby is not breathing.

Specific Medical Emergencies

  • Diabetic Coma: Requires immediate hospital transport.
  • Insulin Shock: If conscious, give sugar (juice, candy). If unconscious, place a small amount of sugar under the tongue.
  • Stroke: Check airway, keep immobilized, and slightly elevate the head/shoulders. Monitor temperature and get help.
  • Seizure: Protect the person from injuring themselves. Do not restrain them. Loosen restrictive clothing.

Officer Safety: Diseases & Transport

Communicable Diseases: Diseases like influenza, hepatitis B, and HIV/AIDS can be transferred via body fluids. Always wear gloves, goggles, and a mask if possible. Wash hands and any exposed skin thoroughly after contact.

Transporting the Injured: The first step is always stabilization. Secure the patient to a stretcher and immobilize injured limbs. Protect them from any sudden movements during transport by ground, air, or water.

Key Term Flashcards

Primary Survey

The initial assessment of a patient to check for life-threatening conditions, following the ABCs: Airway, Breathing, and Circulation.

Shock

(Click to Flip)

A critical condition brought on by a sudden drop in blood flow through the body. Treated by laying the person down and elevating their legs.

Heimlich Maneuver

(Click to Flip)

An emergency procedure for dislodging an obstruction from a person's windpipe by applying a series of abrupt, upward thrusts to the abdomen.

CPR

(Click to Flip)

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation. An emergency procedure involving chest compressions and rescue breaths to manually preserve brain function until further medical help arrives.
