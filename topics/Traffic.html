Traffic Laws and Procedures

A Comprehensive Guide to the Texas Transportation Code

I. Driver's Licenses

General Licensing & Exemptions

  • Sec. 521.021: Prohibits driving without a license.
  • Sec. 521.025: Operators must carry their license and display it on demand to law enforcement.
  • Sec. 521.027 (Exemptions): Includes military personnel operating service vehicles, farm tractor operators, and nonresidents on active duty (and their families) with valid home-state licenses.
  • Sec. 521.029 (New Residents): May use an out-of-state license for only 90 days after becoming a Texas resident.

License Classes & Restrictions

  • Class A (Sec. 521.081): For vehicles with a GVWR of more than 26,000 lbs.
  • Class B (Sec. 521.082): For vehicles over 26,000 lbs or a bus with seating for 24+.
  • Class C (Sec. 521.083): For vehicles with a GVWR less than 26,000 lbs.
  • Class M (Sec. 521.084): For motorcycles or mopeds.
  • Provisional License (Sec. 521.123): Issued to drivers under 18, with restrictions on passengers and late-night driving.

License Violations

Sec. 521.457: Driving with a canceled, suspended, revoked, or expired license is a Class C misdemeanor.

Sec. 521.350: A license suspended for racing is suspended for one year and requires community service.

II. Rules of the Road

General Operation & Speed

  • Sec. 545.051 (Driving on Right Side): Drivers must stay on the right side of the roadway except when passing, avoiding obstructions, or on one-way roads.
  • Sec. 545.062 (Following Distance): Drivers must maintain an assured clear distance to avoid a collision if the front vehicle stops suddenly.
  • Sec. 545.351 (General Speed Rule): Drivers may not travel at a speed greater than is reasonable and prudent for the current circumstances.
  • Sec. 545.352 (Statutory Speed Limits): 30 mph in an urban district, 15 mph in an urban alley, 70 mph on numbered state/federal highways, 60 mph on other highways.
  • Sec. 545.401 (Reckless Driving): Willful or wanton disregard for safety. A misdemeanor.

Stopping, Standing, and Parking

  • Sec. 545.302 (Prohibited Zones): Prohibits parking on a sidewalk, in an intersection, on a crosswalk, on a bridge, on railroad tracks, or within 15 feet of a fire hydrant.
  • Sec. 545.303 (Proper Parking): Must park with right-hand wheels parallel to and within 18 inches of the right-hand curb.

Accident Procedures

  • Sec. 550.021 (Accident Involving Injury or Death): Driver must immediately stop, render aid, and remain at the scene. Failure is a third-degree felony.
  • Sec. 550.022 (Striking Another Vehicle): Driver must stop and exchange information. Failure is a Class B misdemeanor if damage is $200 or more.
  • Sec. 550.062 (Officer's Report): An officer who investigates an accident with injury, death, or damage of $1000+ must file a written report within 10 days.

The posted speed limit. Driving faster than this limit is considered prima facie (on its face) evidence that the speed is not reasonable or prudent.

Authorized Emergency Vehicle
