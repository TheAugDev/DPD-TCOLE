Professionalism and Ethics

The Foundation of Law Enforcement

I. The Law Enforcement Profession

Is Law Enforcement a Profession?

Thinking of law enforcement as a profession encourages higher standards. However, this concept has both advantages and disadvantages.

Advantages of Professionalism

  • Attention to research and best practices.
  • Adherence to agreed-upon standards.
  • Commitment to continuous training and education.
  • Obedience to a strict code of ethics.
  • Leads to excellent service and stronger job commitment.

Disadvantages of Professionalism

  • Requires long and expensive training.
  • Salaries are paid by communities, which may have financial constraints.
  • High standards may limit entry for people from certain socioeconomic backgrounds.

The Law Enforcement Code of Ethics

The code establishes the fundamental duties and principles that every officer must follow.

  • Core Duty: To protect citizens, promote peace, and respect Constitutional rights.
  • Personal Conduct: Maintain excellent moral conduct and obey all laws and department rules.
  • Impartiality: Never allow personal prejudice or bias to influence work.
  • Public Trust: Recognize that public trust is paramount and must be honored.
  • Continuous Improvement: Seize every opportunity to improve performance through education and training.
  • Service Over Self: Subjugate personal desires to the goal of providing top-quality service.

II. Criminal Offenses by Public Servants

Abuse of Office (Texas Penal Code Ch. 39)

  • Sec. 39.02 (Abuse of Official Capacity): A public servant intentionally violates a law related to their office or misuses government property for personal benefit. A Class A Misdemeanor or higher depending on value.
  • Sec. 39.03 (Official Oppression): Unlawfully subjecting another to mistreatment, unlawful arrest, search, or sexual harassment. A Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Sec. 39.04 (Violation of Civil Rights of Person in Custody): Unlawfully denying rights to or having sexual contact with a person in custody. A State Jail Felony.
  • Sec. 39.06 (Misuse of Official Information): Using confidential information obtained through public service for personal gain. A Third-Degree Felony.

Federal Prosecution of Officers (USC 241 & 242)

In certain situations, an officer's conduct can lead to federal charges, which are investigated by the FBI.

  • USC 241 (Conspiracy Against Rights): Conspiring with others to injure, oppress, threaten, or intimidate any person in the free exercise of their constitutional rights.
  • USC 242 (Deprivation of Rights Under Color of Law): An officer using their official position ("color of law") to willfully deprive a person of their rights, privileges, or immunities.
