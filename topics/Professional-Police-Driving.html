Professional Police Driving

Liability, Tactics, and Vehicle Dynamics

I. Legal & Policy Framework

Officer and Agency Liability

An officer's actions behind the wheel carry significant legal consequences for both the officer and their department.

  • Failure to Regard Safety: Officers can be held liable if they fail to drive with appropriate regard for the safety of others on the road.
  • Lack of Policy: Law enforcement agencies can be held liable if they fail to develop a formal, written policy regarding police pursuits.
  • Negligence or Wrongful Acts: If an officer acts with criminal negligence or commits a wrongful act while driving a department vehicle, the department may be held liable.
  • Exigent Circumstances: Liability is higher for accidents that occur when not in direct pursuit of a suspect or responding to an authentic emergency. Emergency driving techniques are only justified in these exigent circumstances.

Pursuit Policies and Emergency Equipment

Departmental pursuit policies outline when to initiate, continue, and terminate a pursuit, as well as the proper use of emergency equipment.

Emergency Equipment Limitations:

  • Lights: Regular headlights can decrease the effectiveness of emergency lights. Visibility is also reduced on bright, sunny days.
  • Sirens: Effectiveness diminishes as vehicle speed increases. They are less audible in heavy traffic, around loud equipment, or in dense urban areas where sound reverberates.

II. Vehicle Dynamics & Officer Skills

Pre-Shift Vehicle Inspection

A thorough pre-shift inspection is mandatory for three reasons: to build officer confidence, maintain the vehicle, and prevent accidents from faulty equipment. The four components are:

  1. Interior Check
  2. Exterior Check
  3. Mechanical Check
  4. Required Inventory Check

The Physics of Driving

Understanding how speed and conditions affect a vehicle is critical for safety and control.

  • Stopping Distance Factors: Influenced by driver skill, vehicle make/model, road conditions, weather, and speed.
  • Effects of Speed: As speed increases, the space needed to turn increases, the risk of exceeding tire traction limits grows, and weight transfer becomes more profound, increasing the risk of rolling over.

Common Causes of Law Enforcement Collisions

Traffic collisions are often preventable and frequently occur due to:

  • Driver Attitude: Overconfidence and impatience are major contributors.
  • Distractions: Use of cell phones or in-car computers diverts attention from the road.
  • Hazardous Maneuvers: Backing up, making left turns, and parking are statistically dangerous for officers.
  • Unsafe Speed: Driving at a speed that is not reasonable and prudent for the current conditions.
  • Fatigue: Slows reaction time and diminishes visual perception.
