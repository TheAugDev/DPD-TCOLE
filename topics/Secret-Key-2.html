Secret Key #2: Make Your Studying Count

Strategies for Effective Learning and Retention

I. Retention: Making Information Stick

Active Recall Techniques

It doesn’t matter how much time you study if you can’t remember the material on test day. The key is to actively recall information rather than passively reviewing it.

  • Spaced Repetition: Check your retention by trying to recall information at later times with minimal prompting. A quick glance at a flashcard is a great way to do this.
  • Self-Quizzing: Regularly ask a friend or study partner to quiz you on the material you've just covered.

Deepening Your Understanding

True retention comes from understanding concepts, not just memorizing facts. Use these methods to solidify your knowledge:

  • Practical Application: Look for ways to put the information into practice. If you use it, you'll remember it.
  • Teach the Concept: Explain a concept you're shaky on to someone else. The act of summarizing, defining, and answering questions will deepen your own understanding.
  • See the Big Picture: Ask yourself how each piece of information fits into the whole subject. When you connect individual concepts, they become easier to remember.
  • Show Your Work: For any multi-step problems, write out each step. This solidifies the process in your mind for easier recall during the test.

II. Modality: Tailoring Your Study Methods

Finding Your Learning Style

"Modality" refers to the method by which you study. Choosing a modality that fits your individual learning style is crucial for effective preparation.

For Visual Learners:

  • Visualize concepts in your mind.
  • Draw images or diagrams to represent information.
  • Use color-coding in your notes.
  • Create symbols that trigger your memory.

For Auditory Learners:

  • Read your notes aloud to yourself.
  • Find a study partner to discuss topics with.
  • Put information into your own words.
  • Record yourself giving a "lecture" on a topic and listen to it later.

Universal Study Tools

Some study tools are effective for nearly every learning style.

  • Flashcards: Excellent for taking advantage of spare moments to review key terms and concepts.
  • Underlining/Highlighting: Helps to identify and focus on key words and phrases. Use different colors for different categories to add a visual element.
  • Mnemonic Devices: Creating acronyms or short phrases can help you remember lists and sequences of information.
