Secret Key #4: Pace Yourself

Mastering the Clock on Test Day

I. The Pacing Strategy

Setting Time Expectations

Your biggest challenge on test day, after knowing the material, is managing your time. Panic can set in even when you have plenty of time left. Developing a pacing strategy builds confidence.

  • Calculate Your Average Time: Divide the total time by the number of questions. If a section has 60 questions and 30 minutes, your hard limit is 30 seconds per question.
  • Set a Goal Pace: Aim for slightly less than your average time (e.g., 25 seconds instead of 30). This builds a "time bank" for harder questions.
  • Stay Calm and Steady: Use your goal pace as a guideline, not a source of distraction. The goal is to avoid spending too much time on any single question.

Handling Difficult Questions

The Rule of Two: Don't Lose Easy Points

Recognize that taking extra time on one question you don't understand may prevent you from answering two questions you *do* understand later in the test.

When your time limit for a question is up:

  • Mark It and Move On: If the test format allows you to return to questions, mark it and come back later if you have time.
  • Make an Educated Guess: If you cannot return, make your best guess and move on. Don't let it distract you from the next question.

Adjusting Your Speed During the Test

If You're Behind Schedule:

  • Don't panic. Speed up wisely by taking a few seconds less on upcoming questions.
  • Quickly eliminate obviously wrong answers to improve your guessing odds.
  • If you can narrow it down to two choices, it is often worthwhile to guess.
  • Don't dwell on questions you had to rush or skip.

If You're Ahead of Schedule:

  • Slow down slightly. Rushing increases the chance of careless mistakes on easy questions.
  • You budgeted time for each question; don't be afraid to use it.
  • Ensure you fully read the question and all answer choices, as more than one may seem correct at first glance.
