Civil Process and Liability

Understanding the Officer's Role in Civil Matters

Core Concepts

Contempt of Court

Contempt of Court refers to actions that disrespect the court's authority. There are two primary types:

General Contempt

Disruptive behavior that undermines the dignity of court proceedings. Penalties vary by court level.

Constructive Contempt

Failure to comply with a direct civil court order (e.g., failure to pay child support). Can result in jail time until the order is obeyed.

The Civil Suit Process

A civil suit begins when a private wrong (tort) cannot be settled. The process involves specific documents and roles that peace officers must understand to perform their duties correctly.

Steps in a Civil Suit:

  1. A civil wrong occurs.
  2. Parties (plaintiff and defendant) are identified.
  3. Plaintiff files a petition with a court of proper jurisdiction.
  4. The court issues a Citation, which directs the defendant on their rights and includes the plaintiff's petition.
  5. The Citation and Petition must be served on the defendant.

Landlord-Tenant Relations & Evictions

Peace officers, especially constables and sheriffs, are directly involved in the eviction process.

  • Notice to Tenant: A landlord must give proper notice before filing for eviction.
  • Eviction Hearing: Held 6-10 days after the tenant is served with the eviction citation.
  • Writ of Possession: If the tenant fails to move out within 5 days after an eviction is upheld, the court issues this writ. It authorizes a sheriff or constable to physically remove the tenant and their property.
  • Lockouts: A landlord may lock out a tenant for non-payment of rent only if the lease allows it and proper written notice is given. The tenant must still be allowed to access their belongings.

Key Term Flashcards

Civil Suit

(Click to Flip)

A legal action initiated by a plaintiff against a defendant based on a private wrong (tort) or dispute.

Citation

(Click to Flip)

An official court document that commands a defendant to appear in court and answer the plaintiff's petition.

Service of Process

(Click to Flip)

The procedure of formally delivering legal documents like citations to the required party. May not be done on Sunday.

Prejudgment Writ

(Click to Flip)

A court order to seize property before a judgment is made, to protect its value during the legal proceedings.

Writ of Possession

(Click to Flip)

A court order authorizing a sheriff or constable to remove a tenant and their property from a residence after an eviction judgment.

Writ of Reentry

(Click to Flip)

A court order that allows a tenant who has been unlawfully locked out to resume residence.
