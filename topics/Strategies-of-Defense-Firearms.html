Strategies of Defense: Firearms

Proficiency, Safety, and Policy

I. Policy and Safe Handling

Departmental Policies

Officers must adhere to their specific departmental policies regarding firearm use in various scenarios. These policies typically govern:

  • Shooting at or from a motor vehicle.
  • The firing of warning shots.
  • The destruction of an animal.
  • The carrying of back-up weapons.
  • The use of off-duty weapons.

Fundamental Skills and Safety

All peace officers must be able to demonstrate proficiency and safety with their issued weapons.

  • Safe Handling: Demonstrate the ability to safely handle both a handgun and a shotgun.
  • Loading/Unloading: Demonstrate the correct process for loading and unloading these weapons.
  • Range Safety: Be familiar with and always follow all rules of the shooting range.
  • Marksmanship: Know the techniques of quality marksmanship, including shooting in daylight, low-light, and nighttime conditions.
  • Weapon Manipulation: Demonstrate holstering and reloading while maintaining eye contact with a potential threat.
  • Maintenance: Demonstrate the ability to properly clean and inspect weapons.

II. TCOLE Firearms Proficiency Standards

Handgun Qualification Course

The handgun qualification course requires a minimum of 50 rounds, and must include at least one timed reload.

Must be able to shoot with two hands.

Shotgun Qualification Course

The shotgun qualification course requires a minimum of 5 rounds fired at a distance of at least 15 yards.

Scoring and Exemptions

The minimum qualification score for both handgun and shotgun courses is 70 percent of the total possible score. In rare cases, peace officers may be granted an exemption from this annual requirement.
